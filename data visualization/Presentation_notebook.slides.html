By Muhammed Jimoh¶

The following variables/features are strongly correlated:

    BorrowerRate, BorrowerAPR, and LenderYield

Hence, I will be using BorrowerAPR to represent the other two, though correlation doesn't necessary implies causation.

  • Yes, the dataset is imbalanced with over one-third of the datset belonging to Completed.
  • Completed: 75.6%, Chargedoff: 20.53% and Defaulted: 3.87%
  • The top five states borrowers reside in are: CA, FL, NY, TX and IL
  • The top two income brackets for borrowers are: 25,000 USD - 49,000 USD, and 50,000 USD - 74,999 USD
  • The 36 months loan term had the highest number of values as expected since the data is imbalanced.
  • Most tend to default on the 60 months loan as it is a long term loan.
  • Alos, the 60 months loan term have a significant amount of chargedoff as compared to others
  • People in the 25,000 USD - 49,000 USD, and 50,000 USD - 74,999 USD tend to borrow a lot.
  • People in the 1 USD - 24,000 USD default on loans alot, and have a significant amount of chargedoff.
  • People with IncomeRange greater than or equal to 25,000 USD had the highest count in terms of loan consideration. With majority being around 25,000 USD - 74,999 USD

  • Most people's reason for borrowing loan is due to Debt Consolidation
  • Majority of the defaulters had oustanding debt already.
  • The major two occupation of borrowers are Other, and Professional
  • The relationship between MonthlyLoanPayment and LoanOriginalAmount is a positive and increasing relationship, though the slope is steeper for different types of term.
  • Most people tend to default on a loan with higher BorrowerAPR as compared to loans with lower BorrowerApr regardless of their employement status.

  • The following pairs of variables are strongly positively correlated:

      [MonthlyLoanPayment, LoanOriginalAmount], [ProsperScore, ProsperRating_Numeric]

  • The following pairs of variable are strongly negatively correlated:

      [ProsperRating_Numeric, BorrowerAPR], [ProsperProsperScore, BorrowerRate],[ProsperProsperScore, BorrowerAPR]
